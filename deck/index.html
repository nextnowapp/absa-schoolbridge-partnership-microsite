Here's the insight the whole proposal turns on. Primary banking relationships are sticky — re-pointing debit orders, salary deposits, saved payees, plain inertia. So the first relationship a young person forms has an outsized chance of becoming the one they keep through their highest-earning decades. If a competitor anchors that at university, the contest is largely over before you enter it. But if you anchor it two-to-four years earlier — at school — you flip that dynamic.
